Architectural applications use slots with metal and wood cabinets, metal studs, and floor-joist trusses. Steel beams are sometimes slotted to allow easier assembly. Slots are commonly shown in cross-section to show their internal details, leaving the length to be dimensioned and shown in another view. Figure 48 shows some examples of slots and grooves drawn in cross-section.

Figure 49 shows an annular groove, another common type of groove machined on the inside of a tube or the outside of a shaft. They’re common for machines that use snap rings to locate gears or pulleys. Groove dimensioning specifies the width (W) and depth (DP)—or the width and diameter—as shown in Figure 49.
