Total runout measures how far an entire surface or feature varies from a referenced datum when it turns 360-degrees. The control frame symbol is two arrows joined at the bottom by a line. Total runout applies to all parts of a feature's surface as measured by the full indicator movement of a measuring device.

It applies to a single line (location) on a circular surface perpendicular to the surface. You would use circular runout when the part's function isn’t critical with respect to its rotational speed as measured by the full indicator movement of a measuring device
