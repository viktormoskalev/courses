There are three types of fits:

  • Allowance - The intentional difference in size between mating parts
  • Feature A specific portion of a part to which the dimensions apply. Features can include surfaces, edges, slots, radii, threads, and chamfers.
  • Basic dimension - The exact theoretical size of a feature, which is used to locate a feature or define a true profile. Basic dimensions are enclosed in a box on the drawing:
    1500

  • Form tolerance -The amount of acceptable surface variation from the perfect form, such as a line, plane, or cylinder.
  • True position -The perfect or ideal location of a feature such as a point, line, or surface.
  • Datum - A geometric figure such as a point, line, plane, or cylinder that’s assumed to be exact for the purpose of a reference.
  • Location tolerance - The tolerance applied to the dimension that allows a specific variation from the perfect location of a feature. The tolerance applies to the relationship of the controlled dimension of a feature from the datum.
  • Profile of a line - The specified permitted variation from the true form of the line.
  • Profile of a surface - The specified permitted variation of a tolerance zone in three dimensions (length, width, and height) or circumference of a feature.
  • Runout - The total variation from a desired circular surface when the part is rotated one full circle around its axis. Runout is often measured with a dial indicator with the part mounted in a rotating fixture (or lathe). If the maximum positive reading of the indicator is +0.001" and the minimum negative reading is –0.002", the total runout would be 0.003".
  • Full indicator movement (FIM) - The term used to replace the former notation of total indicated reading (TIR). Note that the abbreviation FIM isn’t used on drawings.
