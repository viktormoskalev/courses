The all-around symbol—a bent arrow crossing a circle—shows that listed tolerances apply to the surfaces around a control frame feature. Similarly, all-over—represented by an arrow passing through the concentricity double circle—applies the stated tolerance to all surface elements. The between symbol—shown as a double-ended arrow—applies tolerances to adjacent features. Letters will reference the starting and ending points of the between control.
